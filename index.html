Understanding Your Income

Track each income source and see your real take-home pay after tax. Salaried sources estimate tax from the brackets; self-employed sources use the net amount you actually receive.

How to use:

  • Add each source in Settings and choose Salaried or Self-employed.
  • Mark your main earner as Primary.
  • Focus on the Net figures — that's your true spending power.
Learn more
  • Multiple sources: salary, freelance, investments, and side income.
  • Tax: salaried tax is estimated from your progressive brackets; enter Net/Tax per cycle to override it.
  • Pay cycles: weekly, fortnightly, monthly, or yearly are all converted for you.

Managing Your Expenses

Split your spending into Essential needs and Non-Essential wants so you can see exactly where to cut first.

How to use:

  • Add each expense with its real frequency (weekly, monthly, …).
  • When you want to save more, trim Non-Essentials first.
  • Check the per-category weekly and yearly totals.
Learn more
  • Essential: rent, utilities, groceries, minimum debt payments.
  • Non-Essential: dining out, entertainment, subscriptions, hobbies.

Building Your Financial Future

Your savings rate — the share of net income you keep — is the single biggest lever on when you reach financial independence.

How to use:

  • Aim for 20%+ to build wealth steadily.
  • Raise income or cut expenses to lift the rate.
  • Your FI estimate updates from your Settings (multiple + expected return).
Learn more
  • Financial Independence: when your assets cover your expenses.
  • The 4% rule: a common guide that ~25× annual expenses can fund retirement.
  • Compound growth: reinvested returns accelerate the climb over time.

Understanding Your Liabilities

Track what you owe to see your true net worth and decide which debts to clear first.

How to use:

  • Add each debt with its balance and interest rate.
  • Attack the highest-interest debt first (the "avalanche" method).
  • Every dollar of debt paid down raises your net worth.
Learn more
  • High-interest: credit cards, personal loans — clear these first.
  • Secured: mortgages, car loans — usually lower rates.
  • Student / investment debt: often more favourable terms.
